Everything you need to know about this website

For this project, I chose to work on a crafting video because it's the theme that inspired me the most. The video is clear, with different visible steps. For the CSS, I used a color like sand for the background because it’s soft and it looks vintage and artisanal which I thought would go well with the video. The website is quite simple, with clear navigation and large buttons so it’s easy to use. I gave the header and footer are semi-transparent with a slight blur, which gives a modern look, I tried to make it looks like glass. I make sure that the website was responsive so that it works well on both computer and phone. In terms of functionality, I first placed the play bar above the video since it was previously below the video, which I found less pretty. After that, I added the mute/unmute button to the right of the play/pause button, giving the user clear control over the audio. Then I added a full-screen button to the bottom right of the video, and I add the functionality that the video can be in and out of full screen by double-clicking. I also implemented the ability to click anywhere on the progress bar to advance exactly where you want. This gives the user precise control and makes the website intuitive to use. Since I choose a crafting video, I found it interesting to put step buttons from 1 to 5. When you click on a button, it lights up in white so that the user knows exactly which step they are on, and the step buttons direct you to a timecode to the next step for the crafting, which gives the website a dynamic feedback in real time. This interactive UX ensures that the viewer can follow along easily without getting lost, which is particularly useful for crafting tutorials where timing is important. I also paid attention to accessibility and usability: the buttons are large enough to click comfortably, the color contrast between active/inactive steps is clear, and the icons are recognizable and clear enough. The choice of fonts and spacing makes the text readable without distraction. All of these make the experience pleasant and relaxing, like I wanted it to be, and is in line with the crafting theme. The media player is now more than just a video, it became a guided, user-friendly tutorial.
