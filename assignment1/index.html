Thehtml.review

1. What was the first thing you paid attention to when interacting with the experience

The first thing I paid attention to was the bright color, the bubble and the text the center in the center and at the bottom. The website is quite simple which make it very easy to focus on the things you see.

2. Spend two minutes with the experience and create a list of each of your discrete actions.

First, I read the text in the center, then the text at the bottom, and drew on the screen with my mouse. I clicked on the links in the text in the center and it open an other pop up with an other text and other link, I did that with around 10 pop up and I even tried to see if there was something else, like sound (there wasn’t). Then I clicked the on/off toggle, which made all the bubbles appear in the middle of the screen, which surprised me because I didn’t expect so many. I moved the bubbles, closed some of them, and finally clicked on “extra small,” which made all the text bubbles shrink.

3. What part of the experience did you spend the most time engaging with?

The most engaging part clicking on the link in the pop-ups and also moving and closing the text bubbles.

4. What was the most common action in your two minute interaction with the experience?

The most common action I did was reading a lot of text at the start and closing the pop-ups.

5. What is your impression of the intended primary goal of the interactive experience?

I think the primary goal was to create an original experience that invites us to click and explore the site to find things that aren’t necessarily intuitive or usual on more traditional websites.

6. How does the interactive experience communicate this primary goal?

At first glance, the site looks quite empty, which immediately invites us to look at and click on the details like the links in the phrases at the center and bottom.

7. What is your impression of how the experience should be interacted with over time? (For how long and how many different times)

For me, I think this experience is quite short, a few minutes are enough to reach the end, you just have to read everything and dig every link you can see. As soon as you click the on/off toggle, the pop-up in the middle changes to show “The end.”

8. How does the interactive experience communicate how it should be interacted with over time?

Because we first focus on the pop-up in the middle, we instinctively ether close it, read it or open the links. After that, we can draw on the background with the mouse or focus on the phrase at the bottom and click on the underlined actions, the on/off toggle, and “extra small.”

9. What other media forms (digital or otherwise) does the experience reference?

This site can reference traditional websites with pop-ups in digital media, but it can also be compared to other forms of print media, like newspapers, because the text in the bubbles talks about how mobile text is more readable on new media than static text.

10. What does this reference/s communicate to you about how you should act when engaging with your research experience?

For me, this shows that you shouldn’t just focus on digital media but also think about other forms of media. So, you need to explore a bit further, compare, and stay curious to better understand how the form influences the content.

11. What does this reference/s communicate to you about how you should feel when engaging with your research experience?

I think this shows that you should feel open and curious because that way you can discover new things.

12. What is the most frustrating part of the interaction to you and what makes that part frustrating?

The most frustrating part of this site is that you don’t know exactly where to go or click. There are many things to click on, multiple links in each new bubble, and it’s easy to get lost.

13. What is the most satisfying part of the interaction to you and what makes that part satisfying?

For me, the most satisfying part of the site is the first discovery, exploring the site and realizing it’s more complex than it seems, that many buttons lead somewhere or do something. I find this really interesting and it makes you want to discover more.
